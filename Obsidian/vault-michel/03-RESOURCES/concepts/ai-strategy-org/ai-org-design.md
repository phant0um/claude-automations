# AI-Native Organizational Design

The thesis that AI does not merely augment existing organizational structures — it makes traditional hierarchies structurally unnecessary and enables a fundamentally new organizational form.

## Core Argument

Traditional management hierarchies exist to route information between humans at different levels. When AI can query and synthesize organizational knowledge in real time, the primary justification for hierarchy disappears.

> "The architecture of the company will determine its speed." — [[03-RESOURCES/entities/Jack-Dorsey]]

## The Co-Pilot Mistake

Most organizations treat AI as a productivity bolt-on: give each employee an AI assistant, measure productivity gains. This preserves the existing structure.

Dorsey's argument: this misses the structural transformation. The correct move is to rebuild with intelligence at the core — every artifact (docs, decisions, code, communications) becomes queryable intelligence.

## Block's Implementation (2026)

[[03-RESOURCES/entities/Jack-Dorsey]] is restructuring Block from 5 management layers to 2–3, collapsing to three roles:

| Role | Purpose | Enduring human value |
|------|---------|---------------------|
| **IC (Builder/Operator)** | Execution, augmented by agents | Judgment, taste, creativity |
| **DRI (Directly Responsible Individual)** | Owns outcomes + strategy | Accountability, ownership |
| **Player Coach** | Builds others' capacity by doing alongside them | Mentorship, empathy |

Leadership team performs all three roles simultaneously.

## Company-as-Intelligence

When every artifact is queryable:
- Anyone can query the company in real time instead of waiting through a chain of humans
- Meetings shift from slide presentations to working prototypes that can be modified live
- Cost of exploring wrong paths approaches zero

## CEO Role Transformation

| | Old | New |
|-|-----|-----|
| **CEO work** | Define principles; ensure customer context decisions; raise execution bar | Architect of "company-as-intelligence" and its constant alignment |

## System of Intelligence as Market Expression

The [[03-RESOURCES/concepts/ai-strategy-org/system-of-intelligence]] (a16z, Steph Zhang 2026) is the product-market manifestation of the same thesis: the layer that makes GTM knowledge orchestratable and actionable displaces the layer that merely stored it. Where AI org design argues hierarchy becomes structurally unnecessary when knowledge is queryable, the SoI thesis argues SoR incumbents become infrastructure when context can be pulled in parallel across all systems.

Both theses share the same structural logic: intelligence at the core, storage at the edge.
