# Compressed 21st Century

Coined by [[03-RESOURCES/entities/dario-amodei]] in his essay *Machines of Loving Grace*. The thesis that [[03-RESOURCES/concepts/ai-strategy-org/powerful-ai-definition|powerful AI]] will allow humanity to achieve in 5–10 years the biological and medical progress that would otherwise have taken the entire 21st century (~100 years).

## Mechanism

AI acting as a *virtual biologist* — not just a data analysis tool, but one that designs experiments, invents new measurement techniques, controls lab robots, and functions like a Principal Investigator directing graduate students. This accelerates the *entire research loop*, not just data processing.

Key leverage point: ~1 transformative discovery per year (CRISPR, mRNA vaccines, optogenetics, CAR-T, AlphaFold...) drives >50% of biological progress. Returns to intelligence are high for these discoveries because:
- Made by a tiny number of researchers (skill-driven, not random search)
- Many were delayed years by lack of support, not by physical constraints
- Many are independent (parallelizable)

## Expected Scope

- Elimination of most infectious disease
- 95%+ reduction in cancer mortality
- Prevention of Alzheimer's and most genetic disease
- Cures for most mental illness
- Doubling of human lifespan (~150 years)
- Full [[03-RESOURCES/concepts/ai-strategy-org/biological-freedom]]

## Limits

The "compressed century" does not mean instant transformation. Physical experiment latency (cell cultures, animal studies, clinical trials) is irreducible — but massive parallelism is possible on top of it. Amodei estimates 10x acceleration is likely; 100x in 1 year is not.
