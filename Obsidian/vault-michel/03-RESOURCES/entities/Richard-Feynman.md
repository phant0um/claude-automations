# Richard Feynman

American theoretical physicist (1918–1988); Nobel Prize 1965. Known equally for his scientific work and his techniques for learning and understanding.

## Relevance

Two ideas from Feynman are central to [[03-RESOURCES/concepts/learning-cognition/first-principles-reasoning]]:

1. **The Feynman Technique:** explain a concept in simple language; if you can't, you don't understand it — you only know the conclusion. Conclusions can't adapt; understanding can.
2. **Self-deception check:** "The first principle is that you must not fool yourself, and you are the easiest person to fool." Most people fool themselves by repeating conclusions they never derived.
