# Jack Dorsey: Every Company Can Now Be a Mini-AGI

## The Core Thesis

- Hierarchies exist to move information between many humans. AI removes this need.
- Every artifact a company produces is a signal of how it works, builds, and decides.
- Put a layer of intelligence over all those artifacts and you have a model of the company.
- Anyone can now query the company in real time instead of waiting for a chain of humans.
- **The architecture of the company will determine its speed.**

## Why the "Co-Pilot" Framing Misses

- Most of the industry treats AI as something bolted onto the existing company.
- The change is structural, not merely a productivity boost.
- The answer is to rebuild the company with intelligence at the core.

## Block's New Organizational Form

[[03-RESOURCES/entities/Jack-Dorsey]]'s company Block (formerly Square):

- Currently has 5 layers from CEO to any IC — targeting reduction to 2–3 layers this year.
- Collapsing to three roles only:

| Role | What they do | Enduring human skill |
|------|-------------|----------------------|
| **IC (Builder/Operator)** | Augmented by agents; one person now does what a team of 10 used to do | Judgment, taste, creativity |
| **DRI (Directly Responsible Individual)** | Owns customer outcomes, strategy, roadmap | Ownership and accountability |
| **Player Coach** | Builds others' capacity by doing the work alongside them, not just directing | Mentorship and empathy |

Leadership team expected to perform all three roles.

## Roadmap Becomes Emergent

- Traditional roadmap is a guess.
- New expectation: customers request features that don't exist and the system **composes them in real time** from existing capabilities.
- If the system cannot compose, that gap is the roadmap — it emerges automatically from conversation, not from interview transcripts and support tickets.
- Humans apply judgment on whether what customers are asking for is consistent with what the company wants to be.

## The CEO's New Work

| | Old work | New work |
|-|----------|----------|
| CEO | Define principles and team; ensure customer context decisions; raise execution bar | Architect of "company-as-intelligence" and its constant alignment |

## How Meetings Have Changed

- Used to be slide decks and Google Docs.
- Now: everyone arrives with a **working prototype** (simulated or real data) that can be modified live in the room.
- Cost of exploring a wrong path is near zero. Go further first, then commit.
