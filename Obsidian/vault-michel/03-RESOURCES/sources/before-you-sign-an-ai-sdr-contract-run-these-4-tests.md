## Tese central
Every AI SDR is priced on activity. Seats, sends, meetings booked. Whatever pricing rewards is what the product gets good at, and the reward here is the invoice line, not your revenue.

## Argumentos principais
### Test 1: Meeting-to-opportunity conversion
This is the test that decides the rest, so it goes first.
Track every meeting the tool books and measure how many turn into qualified opportunities inside 45 days. **The number you care about is opportunities created, not meetings booked.**
Set the pass line against your own current number, not whatever benchmark the vendor pastes in the deck. If your existing outbound turns 30% of meetings into opportunities and the AI tool pilots at 12%, it can book triple the meetings and still hand you less pipeline.

### Test 2: Signal-triggered or ICP match
Ask the vendor one straight question. What fires a sequence at a given account? If the answer is "they match the ICP filters you set," you are buying a faster spray. If the answer involves a buying signal, you are buying something closer to real targeting.
A meeting booked off an ICP match is a coin flip on intent.
The picture changes when the trigger is something concrete like a funding event, a relevant new hire, a tool added to the stack, or a pricing-page visit. **That buyer is already in motion before you ever land in their inbox.**

### Test 3: Can a human own the handoff
The 60 seconds between "meeting requested" and "meeting confirmed" is the weakest part of the AI workflow, and it's where most no-shows are made.
A prospect who said yes inside an automated thread arrives cold, vague on the call's purpose, and invested in almost nothing. Ask whether the tool lets a human step in there before the calendar invite goes out.
The move that works is small.

### Test 4: Pipeline reported by source
Open the reporting. If the dashboard sums meetings and stops there, you cannot run this evaluation past month one. You need pipeline attributed back to the source that produced it.
The practical bar is whether the tool can push its meetings into HubSpot or Salesforce with enough structure that you can ask, three months later, which sequences and signals produced opportunities that closed.

## Key insights
- AI-booked meetings no-show more, because the prospect agreed to a calendar link inside an automated thread and never had a real exchange first.
- Outbound meetings already no-show around a third of the time, and weak qualification pushes that higher.
- Your AEs burn hours in empty Zoom rooms, and AE time is the most expensive thing in the building. **A demo only captures the moment the meeting books.**
