Businesses do not pay for tools. They pay for outcomes.

You already have a skill that produces an outcome someone wants. Writing. Research. Admin. Making sense of numbers. Explaining things. Keeping people organized.

Claude turns that skill into a service you can deliver on repeat, mostly while you do something else.

These services sell for $2,000 to $5,000 a month. Not because the work is hard, but because the outcome is worth it and almost nobody is offering it yet.

Here are 7 you could build this week from a skill you already have.

## 1\. The Content Engine

The skill: you can write.

The service: a done-for-you content system that turns a client's ideas into a week of posts, emails, and articles.

Claude drafts in the client's voice from one folder of their context. You review and ship.

Sells for $2,000 to $4,000 a month.

## 2\. The Weekly Research Brief

The skill: you are good at finding things out.

The service: a competitor and market brief, delivered to a client every Monday morning.

Claude pulls the sources, scores them, and writes the brief on a schedule. You add the judgment.

Sells for $1,500 to $3,000 a month.

## 3\. The Admin System

The skill: you keep things organized.

The service: a setup that sorts a client's inbox, drafts their replies, and keeps their calendar clean.

Claude connects to their Gmail and calendar and handles the repeat work. You set the rules.

Sells for $2,000 to $4,000 a month.

## 4\. The Live Dashboard

The skill: you can make sense of numbers.

The service: a dashboard that turns a client's messy data into one clean view they actually check.

Claude builds it as a live page and keeps it refreshed. You decide what they see.

Sells for $2,500 to $5,000 to build, plus a monthly retainer.

## 5\. The Outreach Machine

The skill: you are good with people.

The service: a lead system that finds the right prospects and writes a personal first message for each one.

Claude does the research and the drafts. You approve and send.

Sells for $2,000 to $5,000 a month.

## 6\. The Support Assistant

The skill: you are good at explaining things.

The service: an assistant that answers a business's repeat questions in their voice, so their team only handles the hard ones.

Claude runs on their docs as a skill. You keep it accurate.

Sells for $2,000 to $4,000 a month.

## 7\. The Niche Setup

The skill: you know one industry well.

The service: a done-for-you Claude setup for businesses in that niche. Their context, their tools, their workflows, in one folder.

Claude runs it. You are the one who knows what to put in.

Sells for $3,000 to $5,000 to set up, plus monthly.
